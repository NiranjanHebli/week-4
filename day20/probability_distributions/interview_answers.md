## Q1 Conceptual

A normal distribution is any bell-shaped distribution defined by its own mean and standard deviation , so its center and spread can vary depending on the data. A standard normal distribution is a special case of the normal distribution where the mean is exactly 0 and the standard deviation is exactly 1. In standard normal form, each value is expressed as a Z-score, showing how many standard deviations it lies from the mean. This makes comparison across different datasets easier.

## Q3 Conceptual

Hypothesis testing is a statistical method used to decide whether sample data provides enough evidence to support or reject a claim about a population. It helps determine whether an observed result is likely due to chance or represents a real effect.

- Null hypothesis (H0): The default assumption that there is no effect, no difference, or no change in the population.
- Alternative hypothesis (H1): The claim that there is an effect, difference, or relationship, accepted if evidence against H₀ is strong enough.
- p-value: The probability of obtaining the observed result (or more extreme) if the null hypothesis is true. A smaller p-value means stronger evidence against H₀.
- Significance level: A threshold (commonly 0.05) used to decide whether to reject H₀; if p-value < α, the null hypothesis is rejected.
